Overview

Peek App is an unique travel platform that allows users to discover new travel destinations through short-form videos. In addition would also allow to users to save videos to their travel board as well as to book an experiences from the video they're watching.

Project Goal

Design a travel social networking app that help users the to explore and share new travel experiences with other travelers, in addition to facilitating easy bookings.

ROLE

  • Sole UX/UI Designer

TASKS

  • UX Research
  • OOUX
  • Wireflow
  • Wireframe
  • Prototype

DURATION

2 weeks

DISCOVERY PHASE

Understanding the space

For most people discovering new travel destination directly though other travelers is the way to go. That's why it is important to utilize social network aspect of the app as a vehicle to allow user to discover more unique travel experiences. It would also foster more engagement by being more then simply a travel tool but also an engaging social media platform.

In order to get more insights into creating a travel social networking app, I analyzed competitors mobile app from both spaces ranges from Pinterest, TikTok to Airbnb and Culture Trip.

Key findings:

  • Content should be the first thing user see on the app.
  • Map can be a great and intuitive way to discover new experiences.
  • Personalization is very important to improve user experience.
IDEATION

Main features

Explore Location

User should be able to learn more about the attraction or experience in the video. It also allows users to easily book the experience.

Map Integration

With map users can view videos from anywhere. Users can tap on the heatmap on the video selection will update based that area.

Trip Boards

Users can save videos into their travel boards and make a personalized itinerary or a dream vacation list.

OBJECT-ORIENTED UX

Designing with objects in mind

Around the time I started this project I was reading up on Object-Oriented UX by Sopha Voychehovski. With OOUX before focusing on flows and "actions", I prioritized identifying the main objects that makes up an app. This process really helped me to ensure all the elements of the application work well together without creating accidental complexity of unnecessary design elements.

While working through this process I was able to uncover a couple of "entanglements". One is how to prioritize all the CTAs in the "Video" object. With as many as 4 CTAs it is important to properly prioritize the importance of each and translate it into the design. Another thing is the relationship between "Experiences" and "Location. In the beginning I only have the "Experience" object, however upon further consideration I thought that it would be useful to have a "Location" object which provides contextual navigation for the users.

USERFLOW

Mapping out the paths

After completing the OOUX process I started creating a user flow to ensure I have all the user interaction with the main tasks accounted for. The tasks includes quick booking, saving to customizable trip boards, and finding an experience through different paths.

WIREFRAMES

Visualizing the layout

Before building the final design I created wireframes of the main screens. While building the wireframes I was able to brainstorm different layouts that could be the most intuitive the user.

Home

With the main goal of letting users to explore locations/experiences in mind, tapping the "Explore" button will reveal a card that the provides the information of the location with an option to book the experience.

Explore Page

Heatmap allows users to explore all the "hotspots" all around the world. When user tap on a "hotspot" the video selections will dynamically update to correspond to the "hotspots".

Profile

On the profile page the user can also showcase their travel boards with their favorite videos. User can also make them private if they choose to.

REVISION

Give clarity to the main CTAs

After testing the wireframes with a couple of users, I learned that the video CTA's were not clear especially the Explore button. With that information I redesigned the action panel, that priorizes clarity of the 2 main CTAs. which are Explore and Save.
